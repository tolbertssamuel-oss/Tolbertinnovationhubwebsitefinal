Module 1: Foundation

Build exam awareness, grammar essentials, and vocabulary habits.

Module 1 of 6

Module Objectives

  • Understand IELTS format and band scoring rules.
  • Apply core grammar rules to short examples.
  • Create a daily academic vocabulary routine.

Module 1 Quick Start

  • Set your target: Confirm Academic or General Training and your target band (for example 6.5+).
  • Check your level: Complete one timed diagnostic before intensive practice.
  • Build fundamentals: Focus on grammar accuracy, topic vocabulary, and paraphrasing.
  • Follow daily consistency: Study all 4 skills in short, repeatable sessions.

Foundation Study Roadmap (7-Day Plan)

This module gives you a complete beginner foundation. Follow this plan in order and track your work daily.

Day 1: Understand the IELTS System

IELTS measures performance in four skills. Review timing, task types, and band descriptors before practice.

  • Listening (30 minutes)
  • Reading (60 minutes)
  • Writing (60 minutes)
  • Speaking (11–14 minutes)

Confirm the format you need:

  • IELTS Academic (for university admission)
  • IELTS General Training (for work or migration)

Day 2: Take a Diagnostic Test

Complete one timed test to identify your starting band and weak areas. Record your score and top three mistakes in each skill.

Day 3–4: Build Grammar and Vocabulary Core

Focus on sentence structure, verb tenses, articles, connectors, and topic-based academic vocabulary.

  • Grammar drills: 30–40 minutes daily
  • Vocabulary notebook: 20 new words per day with example sentences
  • Paraphrasing practice: 10 sentences per day

Day 5–6: Apply Skills in Guided Practice

Practice each skill with short timed activities to build confidence and speed.

  • Listening: keyword spotting and spelling accuracy
  • Reading: skimming, scanning, and synonym matching
  • Writing: paragraph organization and clear task response
  • Speaking: fluency practice with cue-card responses

Day 7: Review and Improve

Review all mistakes, identify patterns, and set your correction plan for Module 2.

Recommended Daily Study Split

  • 30 minutes Listening
  • 30 minutes Reading
  • 30 minutes Writing
  • 20 minutes Speaking
  • 10 minutes error log update

Common Beginner Mistakes to Avoid

  • Practicing without checking answers and understanding mistakes
  • Memorizing complex vocabulary without correct usage
  • Ignoring timing and leaving sections unfinished
  • Focusing on one skill while neglecting others
